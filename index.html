KYLIE LYNCH ✰

is a junior at the School of Public Engagement. She is in the BPATS (Bachelor program for adults and transfer students) and the Riggio Writing & Democracy Program. Her major is creative writing with a concentration in nonfiction. She is also a transfer student from Miami of Ohio.





INTERVIEW WITH
THE AUTHOR ✰


Interests outside of school?

I'm really interested in yoga and try to do yoga practices at least a few times a week. I love reading and writing and am the blog editor for 12th Street which is one of the New School's literary magazines. I also love walking around the city and exploring new areas of New York. I also love Harry Styles, Twitter, reality TV, cooking, goldfish (the food not the animal lol), my dog, and my weighted blanket.


What's the biggest risk you've ever taken?

I decided to drop out of Miami of Ohio in December 2018 because I was really unhappy and I moved back to London. I ended up working for a fashion magazine part time and selling wedding dresses/doing personal styling part time! I was nervous about taking time off but it allowed me to explore my passions and meet so many incredible people.


What are three words/phrases that you'd use to describe yourself?

Mine would probably be: Enthusiastic, emotional (lol), and easily entertained


What factors lead you to come to The New School?

As I mentioned, I had dropped out of Miami of Ohio and taken a semester off. I knew I wanted to go back to school and I knew I wanted a school with rigorous writing program in a big city. So, the New School ticked off these boxes! I've also always wanted to live in New York, so I figured that now was the perfect time.


What did it feel like when you received your Acceptance Letter to The New School?

I was really excited when I received my acceptance letter! And even more excited when I got my acceptance letter into the Riggio Honors program because I'd heard that the professors were incredible. I didn't have my heart set on the New School yet and was immediately concerned about the cost of the school and the cost of living in New York. Eventually, though, I decided that TNS was the perfect fit for me and I definitely made the right decision.


What was it like leading up to the months before you started classes at The New School? Were there any challenges? Did you have to move?

I did have to move. I moved to New York from London only a couple days before classes started, so I was still jetlagged during my first week. I was nervous to be moving to New York because I didn't know many people, but luckily my best friend from Miami of Ohio had transferred to NYU so we moved in together which has been a lifesaver. As soon as classes started, I was enthralled. I've always loved learning and had missed school while on my gap semester. I especially loved being able to take classes that fit my major, as I had fortunately finished all my pre-reqs in Ohio.


What was your orientation experience like? What were some of your biggest questions?

I actually didn't have orientation. Since I am in BPATS, some of the students are older and work full time, so it's almost impossible to do things during the day. Also, since I'm a transfer student in BPATS (not in Lang or Parsons), I never got emails or correspondence about transfer orientation, which means that I never had the chance to meet transfer students my own age. This is also part of the reason I wanted to become an OL-- to help over BPATS transfer students easily acclimate.


How was your first semester as a New School student? What challenges did you face? What were your favorite moments/highlights?

My first semester went really well. I loved all my classes and could feel myself growing as a student and a person. I'd say that my biggest challenge was trying to meet people. I'm shy at first so it's hard to put myself out there, but I really had to step up and attend events to make friends, even when it was uncomfortable. I live in SoHo when I'm in New York so that's probably my highlight. I love being surrounded by great food, great bookstores, and great shops. I also love the writing program/community at TNS. I feel like I know everyone in my program and they're all so supportive and encouraging.
